The Basics

What is Microsoft Excel?

A quick overview and 5 steps to get started today.

1

Sign In

Go to office.com and sign in with your Nebraska.gov Microsoft account to access Microsoft Excel in your browser, or download the desktop app for the best experience.

2

Explore the Interface

Take a few minutes to click through the main menus and navigation. Hover over buttons to see tooltips — most features are self-explanatory once you explore.

3

Try a Core Task

Jump in and try the most common task — create a document, send a message, or build a simple workflow. Learning by doing is the fastest way to get comfortable.

4

Connect with Your Team

Share your work or collaborate with a colleague. Most M365 apps are built for real-time collaboration — invite someone to work with you early on.

5

Bookmark Help Resources

Save support.microsoft.com and this guide for quick reference. Check the Resources slide at the end for deeper learning paths.

Pro Tips

💡 Use the Search Bar

The search bar is often the fastest way to find features, files, or people — use it before digging through menus.

💡 Right-Click Everything

Right-clicking items usually reveals a context menu with powerful options that aren't visible by default.

💡 Try Dark Mode

Go to Settings → Appearance to switch to Dark Mode — easier on the eyes for long work sessions.

💡 Pin to Taskbar

Pin Excel to your Windows taskbar for one-click access without opening a browser every time.

Keyboard Shortcuts

Essential shortcuts to speed up your Excel workflow.

Ctrl + SSave your work
Ctrl + ZUndo last action
Ctrl + CCopy selected item
Ctrl + VPaste copied item
Ctrl + FFind / Search
Ctrl + PPrint
F5Refresh / Reload
Alt + F4Close window

💡 For the complete list of Excel-specific shortcuts, visit support.microsoft.com/excel
